# FL-01 Summary — AI Workflow Audit & Tool Setup

**Track:** General AI Fluency  
**Week:** 1  
**Estimated Hours:** 4

---

## What I Did

I audited 14 recurring tasks from my actual week — spanning study habits, internship work, and side project development — and classified each one using the framework from Ethan Mollick's *On-boarding your AI Intern*. The four categories I used were: **Just Me**, **Delegate to AI with Review**, **Collaborate with AI**, and **Fully Automate**.

I then set up the required tools (Claude, ChatGPT, Anthropic Academy), created a Claude Project with custom instructions tailored to frontend AI engineering, and completed the first module of *AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations*.

Finally, I selected three recurring tasks to use as the basis for FL-02 through FL-04.

---

## Key Takeaways

- **AI is most valuable as a collaborator**, not a replacement. Most of my tasks (9 out of 14) fell into "Collaborate with AI" or "Delegate with Review" — meaning I stay in the loop and make the final call.
- **Some things must stay human.** Planning my learning goals and attending live sessions are marked "Just Me" because they depend on personal judgment, active engagement, and accountability that AI cannot provide.
- **Only one task is fully automated.** Git commit messages are low-risk and repetitive enough to automate entirely without review.
- **Honest classification matters.** The audit revealed that I often default to doing things myself when AI could meaningfully speed up the work — especially documentation and note-taking.
